Journal of Marketing

The Journal of Marketing is a leading academic journal in the field of marketing. Established in 1936, it is widely recognized for its contributions to marketing theory, research, and practice. The journal aims to advance the understanding of marketing phenomena, consumer behavior, and marketing strategies through high-quality scholarly articles. The Journal of Marketing publishes a wide range of topics related to marketing, including marketing theory, consumer behavior, marketing strategy, advertising, branding, market research, digital marketing, and marketing analytics. The articles published in the journal are based on rigorous empirical research, theoretical developments, and insightful conceptual frameworks. The journal serves as a platform for researchers, academicians, and practitioners to exchange ideas and insights, fostering the development of marketing knowledge and its application in real-world contexts. The Journal of Marketing is known for its interdisciplinary approach, incorporating various perspectives from psychology, economics, sociology, and other related disciplines to provide a comprehensive understanding of marketing phenomena. The articles published in the journal undergo a rigorous peer-review process, ensuring the quality and validity of the research presented. As a result, the Journal of Marketing is highly regarded within the academic community and is frequently cited by marketing scholars and practitioners. Overall, the Journal of Marketing plays a vital role in advancing the field of marketing by publishing cutting-edge research and fostering the exchange of knowledge among researchers and practitioners. It continues to contribute to the development of marketing theory and practice, shaping the way marketing strategies are formulated and implemented in both academia and the business world.
