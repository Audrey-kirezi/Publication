The Great Gatsby

Set against the backdrop of the Roaring Twenties, "The Great Gatsby" is a tale of wealth, love, and the pursuit of the American Dream. The story centers around Jay Gatsby, a wealthy and mysterious millionaire, and his obsessive quest to win back the love of Daisy Buchanan, a married woman from his past. Through the eyes of Nick Carraway, a young man drawn into Gatsby's world, the novel explores themes of social class, illusion versus reality, and the corrupting influence of wealth. F. Scott Fitzgerald's lyrical prose captures the decadence and glamour of the Jazz Age, while also exposing the hollowness and moral decay that lie beneath the surface. "The Great Gatsby" is a timeless examination of the illusions and disillusionments of the American Dream.
